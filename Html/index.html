SOME COMMON DISEASE

Fever

A fever is a temporary elevation in body temperature, often above the normal range of 98.6°F (37°C), indicating the body’s immune response to infections or other health conditions. It can be caused by viruses, bacteria, inflammation, or overheating.

Cough

A cough is a reflex action triggered by the body to clear the throat and airways of irritants, mucus, or foreign substances. It can be dry or productive (with mucus), often caused by infections, allergies, or irritants like smoke.

Cold

A common cold is a viral infection that primarily affects the upper respiratory tract, including the nose and throat. Caused mainly by rhinoviruses, it results in symptoms like a runny nose, sneezing, and mild fever.

Allergies

Allergies occur when the immune system overreacts to harmless substances (allergens) like pollen, dust, or certain foods. This triggers symptoms such as sneezing, rashes, or breathing difficulties, varying from mild to life-threatening (anaphylaxis).

Headache

A headache refers to pain or discomfort in the head or neck region, caused by tension, migraines, sinus pressure, or underlying conditions. It varies in intensity and can be a result of stress, dehydration, or prolonged screen use.
